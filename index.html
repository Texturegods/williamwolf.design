ABOUT ME

Design thinking is anthropological doing - Armonia Alvarado

As a researcher, he is interested in the ways design can be informative to ethnography and anthropology, as well, how ethnography can be informative to design: Does design work help create new concepts and theories? Do design skills have a relevance to ethnographical analysis? The way designers work (often based on a series of in-between objects such as diagrams, mock-ups, and prototypes) and how they see the world around them seem very helpful in reconsidering anthropological matter married to design problems. In his work, he tries to utilize these design skills to uncover new insights.
Utilizing anthropological frameworks and the ethnographic approach provides a holistic data set to implement in the design process. It is no longer feasible in a fast-changing world with technological change growing at an exponential rate for anthropology to be strictly hands off and for design to be more is more. There are too many complex, large scale problems that truly pressure our existence. The design process informed by social scientific research can create a more symbiotic relationship between things, people, agency and culture. Human design researchers attempt to be the arbitrator between human and the things they create.

FASHIONING BLUE COLLARS
MICHAEL TAUSSIG
The more than 5,000-years-old history of indigo-dyed clothing frames a world history of globalisation, labour, fashion, and the best and worst of human nature. From the early to mid 1900s, indigo blue clothing was deeply associated with the working classes, such as the “blue collars” of Australia and American “tradies”. Analysing the values, design, and experience of the blue-collar chambray shirt for the working peoples of Australia and America provides a basis for the shifting views of blue-collar workers today.
